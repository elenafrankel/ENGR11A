Remixing in Tinkercad

This project was split into two parts, one where we remixed designs in Tinkercad, and the other where we remixed designs in Fusion. While I have used Tinkercad before, this was my first experience with Autodesk Fusion. For my first remix, I wanted to design a playing card case in the shape of a heart. I drew the design on paper first to get a baseline idea of what I wanted my design to look like. Then I found a design on thangs.com for a simple playing card case in two parts: a drawer and a sleeve. I exported the STL file into Tinkercad and combined it with a basic heart shape that was included with Tinkercad.

Next I sliced the design in PrusaSlicer and printed the design. The slicer did give me a warning about the structural integrity of the design, but going off of my experience in Project 1, I decided to ignore it and see what would happen. It didn't end up failing, and the printer was able to finish without any supports.

Redesigning and Rethinking

It was pretty easy to figure out how to use the software, especially since I had prior experience. However, when the first print was finished, the two parts of the case did not fit together. I went back to Tinkercad and saw that the sleeve and the heart were two different widths (in the z direction). Due to this inconsistency, the drawer was too tall to fit inside the heart shape, since the heart went through the sleeve in the design, making the sleeve slightly too short. To fix this, I copied the sleeve design and made it a hole instead of a solid, and placed it in the same space that the sleeve was originally, and combined the heart and the rectangular prism shaped hole into one shape. Then I put the solid sleeve back in place. Once again I sliced the design and sent it to the printer, and it was successful on the second try!
